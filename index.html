What I’ll fight for

Ashville has lost its way. We can bring it back with straight answers, responsible management, and department heads held to the same standard as everyone else: honesty and professionalism.

Radical Transparency

Clear updates, open records, and decision-making residents can actually see.

  • Publish meeting materials early and consistently
  • Plain-language explanations of major actions
  • Track follow-through on promises

Honest Department Leadership

Department heads should be leaders—not political favors.

  • Expect truthfulness, accountability, and respect
  • Performance standards with measurable goals
  • Zero tolerance for retaliation or misconduct

Back-to-Basics Service

Residents deserve reliable core services and smart spending.

  • Prioritize streets, infrastructure, and maintenance
  • Budget transparency and cost controls
  • Vendor oversight and fair procurement

Public Safety & Respect

Support our first responders and rebuild community trust.

  • Policies rooted in professionalism
  • Training and equipment managed responsibly
  • Open communication with residents

Smart Growth

Plan for growth that protects neighborhoods and strengthens business.

  • Common-sense planning
  • Support local businesses
  • Improve quality of life

Restore Trust

Leadership must earn trust—every day.

  • Respectful tone from Village leadership
  • Follow the rules and set the example
  • Deliver results, not excuses

My first 100 days

A practical plan to stabilize operations, restore credibility, and get results fast.

1

Open the books

Publish a clear snapshot of budgets, contracts, projects, and priorities.

2

Set expectations for leadership

Standards for honesty, professionalism, and performance—with consequences.

3

Fix the basics

Immediate focus on the services residents feel every day: roads, responsiveness, and communication.

4

Resident-first communication

Consistent updates via website and social, with a simple way to track issues and timelines.

5

Build a results dashboard

Public metrics for projects, response times, spending, and commitments—updated regularly.
